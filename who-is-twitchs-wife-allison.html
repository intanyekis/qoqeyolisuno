Who Is Allison Holker, Stephen “Twitch” Boss’ Wife?

Before Boss died suddenly in December 2022, he married Allison Holker.

Holker was born in Anoka County, Minnesota, and is known for her work as a dancer, choreographer, and actress.

Holker competed on Season 2 of So You Think You Can Dance, just like Boss, and was an All-Star in Seasons 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, and 14.

Holker has acted in a number of shows, such as High School Musical (2006), High School Musical 2 (2007), and Footloose (2011).

Holker now hosts the shows Design Star: Next Gen on HGTV and Disney’s Fairy Tale Weddings on Disney+.

She Speaks Up About His Death

In a heartbreaking statement on Wednesday, December 14, Stephen “Twitch” Boss’s wife, Allison Holker, confirmed that he had died.

“It breaks my heart to tell you that my husband Stephen has left us,” Holker, who is 34, said in a statement to people. “Stephen made every room he walked into brighter.” He cared most about his family, friends, and community. Leading with love and light was the most important thing in the world to him. He was the strongest part of our family, the best husband and father, and an inspiration to his fans.

Boss and Holker married in 2013 and had three children together: Weslie, Holker’s 14-year-old daughter from a previous relationship, was adopted by Boss, along with Maddox, 6, and Zaia, 3.

Us Weekly confirmed earlier on Wednesday that the former DJ on the Ellen DeGeneres Show died Tuesday, December 13, by suicide. He had turned 40.

“To say he left a legacy would be an understatement, and his positive effects will continue to be felt,” Holker said on Wednesday. “I am sure that there will never be a day when we don’t remember him.” “We’d like to be left alone during this hard time, both for me and especially for our three children.”

She ended her speech with a message to her husband: “Stephen, we love you, we miss you, and I will always save the last dance for you.”

Boss and Holker celebrated their ninth wedding anniversary on December 10, three days before she told police that her husband had left their home without his car.
