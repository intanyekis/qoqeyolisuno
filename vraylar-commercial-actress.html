As the plot develops, Vraylar is suggested as a means of enhancing the effects of antidepressants.

The ad emphasizes that Vraylar has been clinically demonstrated to help treat overall symptoms of depression more successfully than antidepressant alone when paired with an antidepressant regimen.

Clinical investigations on Vraylar support this claim by demonstrating that, for the majority of users, the medication efficiently decreases symptoms of depression without having a substantial impact on weight.

However, the TV ad also addresses a few important safety concerns. It warns against odd behavioral changes or suicidal thoughts and calls attention to the increased risk of death or stroke among elder dementia patients.

It also includes a warning about the potential for antidepressants to increase these risks in children and young adults.

The TV spot also warns viewers of lethargy, restlessness, nausea, dizziness, increased appetite, and tiredness as potential side effects of Vraylar.

It highlights how important it is to report any concerning symptoms that could be indicators of potentially fatal conditions, such as fever, tense muscles, confusion, or uncontrollable jerky muscle movements.

The actress remarks, “I didn’t have to adjust my treatment,” regarding her positive experience with Vraylar in spite of any potential side effects.

I only assisted with it.” This lends credence to the notion that Vraylar can provide substantial relief without necessitating a complete alteration of a patient’s treatment plan.
